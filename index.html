Abstract

Reconstructing dynamic scenes from monocular videos remains challenging because motion modeling methods often suffer from temporal instability, boundary artifacts, or geometric collapse. We propose a principled motion decomposition framework for monocular dynamic scene reconstruction based on dynamic Gaussian Splatting. The method separates motion into two complementary components: a Chebyshev field for globally stable low-frequency motion trends and a Residual Motion field for localized high-frequency dynamics outside the spectral subspace. The joint optimization objective enforces appearance consistency, geometric plausibility, and residual regularization, producing stable and faithful dynamic reconstruction.
